# Market Impact Framework

Use this framework to decide whether a story belongs in a financial news brief.

## High Impact

- Central bank rate decisions, inflation surprises, employment data, liquidity facilities, or emergency policy actions.
- Major regulatory or legal actions affecting listed companies, exchanges, banks, brokers, insurers, fintech, crypto, or cross-border capital flows.
- Earnings, guidance, capital raises, buybacks, dividends, accounting issues, defaults, credit events, or management changes at market-moving companies.
- Geopolitical actions that affect supply chains, sanctions, export controls, tariffs, shipping routes, energy, chips, rare earths, or defense.
- China semiconductor policy, U.S. export controls, entity list changes, advanced equipment restrictions, major foundry capacity news, AI chip availability, or domestic substitution breakthroughs.
- Broad market stress: bank funding pressure, sharp currency moves, bond market dislocations, commodity shocks, major index rebalances, or exchange outages.

## Medium Impact

- Sector policy signals without immediate rules.
- Analyst or institutional commentary that changes consensus expectations.
- Product launches or technology milestones with plausible revenue, margin, supply chain, or capex implications.
- M&A rumors from credible sources, preliminary investigations, or early-stage trade negotiation news.

## Low Impact

- Routine executive interviews without new numbers or policy details.
- Repackaged articles repeating already-known facts.
- Opinion pieces without new evidence.
- Social media speculation, unless corroborated by credible sources.

## Scoring Hints

Score up when the story has:

- Direct effect on revenue, margins, financing cost, valuation multiples, liquidity, or regulatory permission.
- Clear affected assets: indexes, sectors, tickers, bonds, FX, commodities, or supply-chain nodes.
- Official documents, named sources, filings, or primary data.
- Surprise versus market expectations.

Score down when the story has:

- Vague sourcing.
- No identifiable asset or sector channel.
- Only sentiment impact with no follow-up catalyst.
